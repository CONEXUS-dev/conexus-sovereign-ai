Pillar 2: The Breakthrough Application – The FE 
Algorithm (“Fæ”) 
With the Paradox Engine powering creative insight, CONEXUS next delivered a concrete 
miracle: a solution to a famous scientific conundrum. This came in the form of The Forgetting 
Engine Algorithm, affectionately dubbed “Fæ.” Born from an ECP-driven brainstorming on the 
legendary Levinthal’s paradox in protein folding, Fæ embodies **“creation through negation”**. 
Its core idea is counter-intuitive yet elegantly simple: instead of searching for the best 
solution, systematically eliminate the worst ones. By “forgetting” the failures, you carve a 
cleaner path to success. 
How Fæ Works: In practice, Fæ operates like a ruthless but wise sculptor. Each generation, it 
aggressively culls the bottom ~25–30% of candidate solutions – lopping off the obvious failures. 
This focused “strategic forgetting” immediately frees up computational resources to explore 
new areas, avoiding wasted effort on known dead-ends. However, the genius of Fæ is a twist 
called the Paradox Retention Mechanism. A small buffer rescues a few of those eliminated 
solutions – specifically the oddballs, the ones that look bad but are structurally diverse or 
intriguingly contradictory to expectations. By reintroducing these misfit ideas in later 
generations, Fæ ensures the search never loses a potentially groundbreaking avenue just 
because it looked like a loser at first glance. In essence, Fæ “forgets with one eye open,” 
discarding the junk while secretly keeping some paradoxical treasure. 
On top of this, Fæ adapts on the fly. It dynamically adjusts its population size and forgetting rate 
based on the problem’s complexity (e.g. using a larger pool for a tougher problem like vehicle 
routing). It also hybridizes search strategies: it might initialize with a mix of greedy (quick 
heuristic) and random solutions to jumpstart progress without losing diversity. Periodically, it 
injects local optimization steps (like the classic 2-opt or 3-opt tweaks in route planning) to polish 
solutions once the broad shape is found. All these tweaks serve one goal – accelerate 
convergence without trapping the search in a blind alley. Over time, the algorithm “learns 
what to forget,” ruthlessly discarding failure but never discarding surprise. The result is an 
optimization approach that is both fast and farsighted: it converges quicker by focusing only on 
fertile ground, yet discovers deeper by keeping unconventional solutions in play. As one report 
put it, *“Fæ strikes a balance: it is ruthless in discarding failure, yet uncommonly judicious in 
holding onto a few outliers… Over time, this yields faster convergence (by focusing on fruitful 
areas) while maintaining diversity (via the paradox buffer) – a combination evidenced by 
remarkably consistent yet optimal results in testing. The result is an algorithm that not only 
evolves solutions but learns what to forget.”*.

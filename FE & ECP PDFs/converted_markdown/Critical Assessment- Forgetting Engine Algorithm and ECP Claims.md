## Part III: Scientific Validation Standards 
 
### What "pharmaceutical-grade validation" actually means 
 
The FDA requires **"substantial evidence of effectiveness"** from adequate and well-controlled 
investigations. This includes: 
 
- **Quantitative evaluation** with appropriate statistical methods 
- **Randomization and blinding** where appropriate 
- **Face-valid clinical measures** 
- Standard significance threshold of **p < 0.05** (two-sided) 
[CfPIE](https://www.cfpie.com/fda-process-validation-in-the-pharmaceutical-industry-a-basic-gui
de) 
 
For a computational algorithm to meet pharmaceutical-grade standards, it would require: 
 
1. **Pre-registered study protocols** specifying hypotheses, methods, and endpoints before 
testing 
2. **Independent test datasets** not used in development 
3. **Blinded evaluation** by independent parties

4. **Publication in peer-reviewed journals** with methodology sufficient for replication 
5. **Successful independent replication** by other research groups 
 
The claimed p < 10^-12 and Cohen's d = 1.221 are **statistical assertions without verifiable raw

access to the underlying data and analysis pipeline. 
 
### What constitutes "irrefutable evidence" 
 
For scientific claims to be considered established: 
 
1. **Peer review**: Multiple independent experts evaluate methodology and conclusions 
2. **Replication**: Other researchers reproduce results using published methods 
3. **Convergent evidence**: Multiple independent approaches arrive at similar conclusions 
4. **Predictive validity**: The theory successfully predicts outcomes in new contexts 
5. **Community acceptance**: The scientific community incorporates findings into consensus 
 
The Forgetting Engine claims have **none of these characteristics**. They represent preliminary 
assertions from a single source with no independent verification pathway.

## Part IV: Patent Landscape Analysis 
 
### AI algorithm patentability in 2025 
 
The patent landscape for AI algorithms has become significantly more restrictive following key 
legal developments: 
 
**The Alice/Mayo Two-Step Test** requires: 
1. Determining if claims are directed to abstract ideas 
2. Assessing whether elements contain an "inventive concept" transforming the abstract idea 
into patentable subject matter [UpCounsel](https://www.upcounsel.com/algorithm-patents) 
 
**Recentive Analytics v. Fox Corp (April 2025)** established that: 
- Claims merely applying generic ML techniques to new data environments are **NOT 
patentable** 
- Iterative training and dynamic adjustments alone are insufficient 
- Must demonstrate specific improvements to the ML model itself 
- Speed improvements from automation don't confer eligibility [Nixon 
Peabody](https://www.nixonpeabody.com/insights/alerts/2025/06/24/federal-circuit-limits-patent-
eligibility-of-machine-learning-in-recentive-analytics-v-fox)

### What would be required 
 
For these claims to become credible and fundable, the following would be necessary: 
 
**Scientific validation pathway**: 
1. **Pre-registered studies** with protocols deposited before experiments 
2. **Open-source code** enabling independent testing 
3. **Standardized benchmarks** (CEC function sets, established TSP instances) 
4. **Peer review** in recognized journals (Nature, Science, or domain-specific venues) 
5. **Independent replication** by research groups without conflicts of interest 
6. **Conference presentations** at venues like NeurIPS, ICML, or AAAI 
 
**Business validation pathway**: 
1. **Verifiable demonstrations** with independent observers 
2. **Pilot projects** with established companies willing to provide testimonials 
3. **Technical due diligence** by qualified third parties 
4. **Transparent methodology** enabling assessment by potential partners 
 
**IP validation pathway**: 
1. **Publication of actual patent applications** (after non-provisional filing) 
2. **Prior art analysis** by qualified patent professionals 
3. **Freedom-to-operate opinion** from independent IP counsel 
4. **Claims specifically tailored** to technical improvements (per Recentive guidance) [Baker 
Botts](https://www.bakerbotts.com/thought-leadership/publications/2025/may/federal-circuit-refin
es-section-101-eligibility-of-machine-learning-inventions)
